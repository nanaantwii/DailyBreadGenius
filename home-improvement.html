Wall Nail Trick

Before hammering a nail into the wall, place a small piece of painter's tape over the spot to prevent paint chips.

Watch this video to learn how to use tape to easily hammer a nail into the wall:

Step By Step Guide:

    • Step 1: Gather your materials: a bar of soap and a hammer.
      Step 2: Take the bar of soap and rub it over the nailhead. This will help lubricate the nail and make it easier to drive into the wall.
      Step 3: Position the nail at the desired location on the wall and hold it firmly in place with one hand.
      Step 4: With the other hand, carefully hammer the nail into the wall at a slight angle. The soap will help the nail slide into the wall more smoothly.
      Step 5: Once the nail is in place, remove the soap residue with a damp cloth.
      Step 6: Hang your picture frame, shelf, or other object on the nail, and ensure it's level.

    • Furniture Scratch Fix

    Use a walnut to rub out scratches on wooden furniture. The natural oils will help restore the wood's appearance.

    Watch this video to learn how to rub out scratches on furniture using walnuts:

    Step By Step Guide:

    • Step 1: Clean the scratched area with a soft cloth and mild soap to remove any dirt or debris.
      Step 2: Assess the depth of the scratch. If it's shallow, it can usually be fixed with a few simple methods.
      Step 3: For light scratches, apply a small amount of furniture polish or oil directly onto the scratch and gently rub it in with a soft cloth in a circular motion.
        Step 4: For deeper scratches, use a furniture touch-up pen or marker that matches the color of your furniture. Apply the marker or pen to the scratch, filling it in completely, then gently wipe away any excess with a soft cloth.
        Step 5: For very deep scratches or gouges, you may need to use a wood filler. Apply the filler to the scratch with a putty knife, making sure to overfill slightly. Once dry, sand the area smooth and touch up with paint or stain to match the rest of the furniture.
        Step 6: Finish by applying a thin coat of furniture wax or polish to the entire piece of furniture to restore its shine and protect the repaired area.
